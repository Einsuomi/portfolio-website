# Luxembourg International Schooling Options

**Type:** family
**Access:** public
**Tags:** Luxembourg, education, international-school, children, European-baccalaureate
**Updated:** 2026-05-08
**tldr:** Six free state public international schools in Luxembourg offer French/German/English tracks leading to the European Baccalaureate — strongest education argument for the relocation. Annual application window is March 1–15 for September entry.
**Related:** [[relocation-decision-luxembourg]], [[personal-knowledge-management]]

## Summary
Luxembourg offers an exceptionally diverse international schooling landscape, driven by nearly 48% of its population being non-nationals representing 170 nationalities. Free state European schools offer French, German, and English sections leading to the European Baccalaureate — a qualification recognized for university admission across every EU country. For a family relocating to Luxembourg with school-age children, the education system is one of the strongest arguments in favor of the move.

## Detail

### Why Luxembourg's Education System Is Unusually Strong for Expat Children
The country's size and international workforce have forced investment in multilingual, internationally-recognized schooling that most countries simply don't need. The result is that a child in Luxembourg can realistically graduate with French, German, English, and Luxembourgish — a linguistic portfolio that opens universities across Western Europe.

### Free State European Schools (recommended starting point)
Six state-run European schools offer education from nursery through secondary, at no cost, open to all residents:
- **EIDE** (Differdange & Esch-sur-Alzette) — French, German, English sections
- **LLIS** (Junglinster) — French, German, English sections
- **LESC** (Clervaux) — French, German, English sections
- **EIMAB** (Mersch) — French, German, English sections, boarding available
- **EIGT** (Gaston Thorn) — French, German, English sections
- **EIMBL** (Mondorf-les-Bains) — French, German, English sections

All lead to the **European Baccalaureate**, recognized for university admission across all EU countries. Luxembourgish is compulsory through the third year of secondary.

**Enrolment cycle (confirmed for EIGT and EIDE 2026 cycle):**
- Application window: **March 1–15** each year, online via the school's website
- Admission decisions: by mid-May
- Entry: September of the same year
- L1 placement principle: students are placed in the language section corresponding to their mother tongue / dominant language; English section is the standard route for relocating English-speaking expat families
- Mid-year admission: not the standard path — only on exceptional/waitlist basis, dependent on capacity. Plan around the September entry cycle.

### French-Track Private Options (if preferring the French system)
- **Lycée Vauban** — follows the French national curriculum; leads to the French Baccalauréat, giving direct access to French universities including Sciences Po and grandes écoles
- **École Charlemagne** — French primary

### English-Track Options
- **International School Michel Lucius (ISML)** — British curriculum, Cambridge-accredited; leads to IGCSE → AS/A-levels
- **St George's International School** — English
- **International School of Luxembourg (ISL)** — IB in English

### International Baccalaureate (IB)
Both state and private schools offer IB in French or English, starting from secondary. Recognized in 150 countries.

### European Schools (EEL I and EEL II) — distinct from the state public international schools
Two intergovernmental European Schools in Kirchberg (EEL I) and Bertrange/Mamer (EEL II), primarily for children of EU institution staff (who have priority). For non-EU-staff applicants, these are **fee-paying** at roughly €10–20k/year per child. **Do not confuse with the free state public international schools (EIDE, EIGT, etc.)** — the naming is genuinely confusing because both are based on the European Schools curriculum and lead to the European Baccalaureate.
