### Activity shortlist (not yet booked)
- **Tabarca Island catamaran cruise** — 6 hr round trip from Alicante port (Maxi catamaran next to bar Noray, in front of Mundo de los Niños). 11:00 departure, includes paella + drinks, snorkel gear. 4.7/5 across 892 reviews. Reviewer note: not great for kids under ~3. Tabarca is Spain's first marine reserve and a rare clear-water spot reachable directly from Alicante. [Source: GetYourGuide listing]
- **Whale & dolphin watching with Pura Aventura** — Osprey II boat, departs **Marina Miramar, Puerto Deportivo Santa Pola** (NOT Alicante port; Santa Pola is ~25 min south of Alicante). 8:55 → 14:00, includes a cove swim stop. Dolphin sighting "guaranteed" with a voucher refund if no sighting. [Source: puraventuraspain.com]
- **Pedalo with slide** — walk-up rental, no booking needed, available at rental kiosks on all major Spanish beaches (e.g. Playa de San Juan). Slide drops straight into the sea.
- **Tabarca catamaran cruise (6hr)** — 11:00 departure from Alicante port. Paella + drinks + snorkel gear included (mask + fins; no snorkel tube — buy €5 on board or bring own). 3 hours on the island covers both snorkeling and the village (18th-century walls built by King Charles III, small baroque church, lighthouse). Catamaran much better than speedboat for a child: stable deck, less motion sickness risk, space to move. Book in advance for July.

**Honest Tabarca snorkeling expectations:** marine-reserve status means more fish density, but "rays, barracuda, sea turtles" is marketing copy. What you'll actually see: schools of colourful fish (bream, mullet), sea urchins, octopus hiding in rocks, good 20m+ visibility. For a child, that's already genuinely exciting — the "wow" bar at that age is much lower than the marketing list.

### Sea-clarity geography — where the jelly-sea actually is
Alicante city beaches (Postiguet, San Juan) are urban sand — pleasant but not glass-clear. The genuinely turquoise water is in rocky coves **north of Alicante**, plus Tabarca to the south.

**Top clear-water coves, ranked:**

| Rank | Cove | Municipality | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Cala del Moraig** | Benitatxell (between Moraira & Jávea) | Underwater freshwater spring (Falla del Moraig) mixes cold freshwater + seawater → milky-turquoise color. Cova dels Arcs sea cave at south end. |
| 2 | **Cala Granadella** | Jávea | National Geographic best beach in Valencia region. Deep turquoise, pine-backed pebble cove, 2 cove restaurants, snorkel/dive. Parking fills by 10 am summer. |
| 3 | **La Barraca / Cala Portitxol** | Jávea | Glassy turquoise with view of Portitxol islet. Most photogenic spot in Jávea. |
| 4 | **Cala Blanca / La Caleta** | Jávea | Closest to town, white pebble, easy snorkel. |
| 5 | **Cala Sardinera** | Jávea | Most secluded, 15-min hike-in, no facilities. |
| – | **Cala Ambolo** | Jávea | Officially closed (landslide risk); cliff viewpoint only. |

### Car-free reality
- **Altea** and **El Campello** are truly car-free options (TRAM Line 9 / Line 3 respectively).
- **Jávea** is partial: walkable within Arenal zone, but the famous coves have no regular bus — taxi or one-day car rental needed. Boat tours from Jávea port are a valid alternative (hit Granadella + Portitxol + Cova Tallada from the water).
- **Moraira / Cumbre del Sol** require a car the whole week.
- **Car-rental pattern:** For a hybrid Jávea base, renting a car just **2 days** (one cove day + one Santa Pola whale-watching/Tabarca day) is often cheaper than repeated cove taxi trips and gives full flexibility for those two days.
- **Important:** At major Spanish rental companies (Hertz, Sixt, Avis, Europcar), paying deposit by **debit card** means €1,500–2,500 of actual cash frozen for 2–4 weeks. Some companies refuse debit entirely. Budget chains (Goldcar, Centauro) may allow debit but have a poor reputation. Having a credit card for the deposit pre-auth (~€300–500, released quickly) is practically necessary for comfortable car rental in Spain.

### Standards comparison — Alicante vs Jávea
Different products, not better/worse. For the slow-sun-sea brief, **Jávea is the higher-quality match** and worth the price premium:
- Beach quality: Jávea (clearer water, framed by cliffs)
- Dining ceiling: Jávea (Michelin presence vs Alicante's wider mid-range)
- Crowds: Jávea calmer
- Hotel breadth & price: Alicante wider/cheaper
- Things to do beyond beach: Alicante (city culture)
- Authentic Spanish feel: Alicante (Jávea is expat-bubble)
